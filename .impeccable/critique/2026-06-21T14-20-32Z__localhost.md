## Overall Impression

The copy is exceptional — this is the most distinctive brand asset on the site and it is being underserved by a visual system that has let regressions slide (gradient text, side-stripe blockquotes) and a structure that buries its most distinctive feature (the Architect experience). The foundation is genuinely strong. The biggest opportunity is not visual reinvention but clearing the debris: fix the four absolute ban violations, restructure the About page bio, and make the Architect experience discoverable on repeat visits.

## What's Working

1. The copy voice is exceptional and rare. "Tell me what you're actually trying to solve. If I'm not the right person for it, I'll tell you that too" is a trust signal that costs nothing and buys everything. This voice could carry an entire brand on its own.

2. The homepage hero-to-services sequence. The 200/900 weight split on Duncan/Boyne, staggered animation timing (0.1s-0.45s), and the immediate gold services block create genuine identity rhythm that would not look out of place on an award-shortlisted agency site.

3. The one-signal gold rule is largely respected. The two-level surface/bg split creates readable banding without jarring jumps. Most of the site respects "gold means something" — the violations are specific and fixable.

## Priority Issues

### [P0] Gradient text in Hero.svelte:158 — absolute ban violation
**Why it matters**: background-clip: text combined with a gradient background is an explicit absolute ban. A gradient-clipped text element on a portfolio that rejects AI-bro aesthetics is a direct brand contradiction.
**Fix**: Remove background-clip: text and the gradient. Replace with solid --color-accent for the gold word, matching every other heading on the site.
**Suggested command**: /impeccable polish

### [P0] Side-stripe blockquotes in 4 content templates — absolute ban violation
**Why it matters**: border-left: 4px solid var(--color-accent) appears in blog/[slug], talks/[slug], reading/[slug], and anime/[slug] — every content detail template. 7 detector hits. Side stripes > 1px as colored accent are an absolute ban. Using the signal color as a decorative stripe dilutes the one-signal rule on every piece of content.
**Fix**: Replace with a full top-border rule (1px --color-border), a background tint (--color-accent at 5% opacity), or pull-quote text at larger size with no border.
**Suggested command**: /impeccable polish

### [P1] Eyebrow labels appear on every page and inside every section
**Why it matters**: The brand rules explicitly ban tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section. Contact page has three eyebrows. Services uses .context-label. About uses .section-label. When the device is everywhere it signals nothing — it becomes AI scaffolding grammar, not brand voice.
**Fix**: Reserve the eyebrow for three uses maximum across the entire site. Remove all in-section eyebrows. Let the 900-weight display headings carry the page.
**Suggested command**: /impeccable distill

### [P1] About page bio structure buries the trust signal
**Why it matters**: For a Norfolk SME decision-maker evaluating whether to hire Duncan, the bio lists Pokemon, parenting, a border collie, AuDHD, and community founding at equal visual weight in five continuous unbroken paragraphs. The AuDHD disclosure — a genuine professional differentiator explaining Duncan's clarity-first working style — lands in paragraph 4 between recreational anecdotes, where it reads as a personal detail rather than a working philosophy.
**Fix**: Restructure into three visually separated sections (work story, human story, community story) without eyebrow labels — use a thin --color-border rule or size shift. Pull the AuDHD paragraph to the opening of the human story or to a standalone pull-quote. Reframe it as working philosophy, not personal disclosure.
**Suggested command**: /impeccable layout

### [P1] Architect experience is invisible to mobile users, repeat visitors, and reduced-motion users
**Why it matters**: The site's single most distinctive asset is gated behind WebGL2, first-visit detection, and motion capability checks. Mobile users (likely 40%+ of traffic) see a black screen and skip. Repeat visitors never see it again. The ?experience=1 replay param exists in gate.ts:19 but is entirely undiscoverable. The brand peak is invisible to the majority of visitors.
**Fix**: After skip or on repeat visits, embed a short looping video or animated still of the cathedral on the homepage with a "Re-enter the Architect" link (?experience=1). Transforms a one-time gate into a persistent brand signal at zero additional development cost.
**Suggested command**: /impeccable shape

### [P2] Hover-expand vs. click-expand inconsistency — keyboard accessibility failure
**Why it matters**: Homepage and blog rows expand on CSS :hover. Talks uses click-expand with a chevron. The hover pattern means row-excerpt, svc-desc, and related content are unreachable by keyboard. Sam (accessibility user) cannot access blog excerpts on the homepage. Casey (mobile user) also loses this content since hover does not fire on touch.
**Fix**: Standardise on click-expand with visible chevron. Replace CSS :hover grid expansion with Svelte open state toggle on click, matching the Talks implementation. Add chevron affordance to homepage rows.
**Suggested command**: /impeccable audit

## Questions to Consider

1. Who is the Architect experience actually for? The experience runs for first-time desktop visitors with WebGL2 and sufficient hardware. The primary commercial target will likely see a black loading screen and skip. Is it optimised for impressing peers or converting freelance clients?

2. Why is there no pricing page? The Services page describes work but not the transaction. For SME clients, the absence of even a day-rate range creates friction — the first enquiry is about cost, and if the site does not orient that expectation, conversations stall at the contact form.

3. The AuDHD disclosure is either a differentiator or a personal detail depending entirely on placement. Current placement (paragraph 4 of 6, between Pokemon and community founding) makes it a personal detail. "Clarity is kindness — especially in data and design" is a professional philosophy. Which is it meant to be?
