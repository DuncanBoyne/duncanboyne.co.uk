# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary audience is prospective freelance clients who need Power BI, dashboard design, automation, or practical AI strategy from someone who understands business data in the real world. They are likely SME and mid-market leaders, finance teams, manufacturing teams, operations teams, and local Norfolk / Norwich / Great Yarmouth businesses who need clear decisions, not another unused report.

The secondary audience is event organisers, community leaders, and technical practitioners who need a credible speaker, educator, or collaborator across Power BI, the Power Platform, AI, data storytelling, and accessibility.

The tertiary audience is the wider Power BI and AI community: readers, learners, contributors, and people looking for resources, open-source work, talks, or a human point of view.

## Product Purpose

This site positions Duncan Boyne as a Power BI freelancer, AI strategist for Tugger (www.tuggerapp.com), community leader, speaker, and educator.

The site should turn credibility into momentum: make it obvious what Duncan does, who he helps, why he is different, and how to contact him. It should support consulting leads, speaking invitations, community trust, and education in public without flattening him into a generic consultant profile.

Success looks like the right people quickly understanding the offer: dashboards people actually open, automation that removes pointless manual work, AI strategy grounded in trustworthy data, and education that is useful because it has real scars behind it.

## Brand Personality

Blunt, useful, human, design-first, accessible.

The brand should feel like a practitioner who has done the work, broken things, fixed them, and can explain the lesson without pretending it was cleaner than it was. It should carry the warmth of community leadership and teaching, but with enough edge to reject grift, fluff, and "AI will fix everything" nonsense.

The Tugger connection should strengthen the AI strategy signal without taking over the whole site. Duncan is not just a Power BI delivery person; he is moving into the overlap between BI, AI, product thinking, and practical business strategy.

## Anti-references

- Generic corporate consultancy websites with stock handshakes, vague transformation language, and no human voice.
- Microsoft-default blue dashboard aesthetics as the whole visual identity.
- AI-bro thought leadership, template authority, fake certainty, and "secret formula" grift.
- Glassy SaaS gradients, purple AI blobs, and over-polished startup pages that could belong to anyone.
- Keyword-stuffed local SEO copy that reads like a Google search out loud.
- Pretty but unreadable design: low contrast, tiny text, mystery navigation, decorative motion, or inaccessible visual hierarchy.
- Overbuilt dashboard culture: 200 visuals, endless slicers, and no clear answer.
- Sterile CV portfolio pages that list achievements without showing judgment, teaching, community, or personality.

## Design Principles

- Clarity is kindness. Every page should help someone understand the offer, the person, or the next action without decoding a brand puzzle.
- Useful beats impressive. The site can be polished and cinematic, but usefulness wins over decorative cleverness every time.
- Show the scars. Copy, talks, and project descriptions should reveal the messy problem, the decision made, and the lesson learned.
- Community is proof. Speaking, user groups, summits, open source, and education are not side content; they are evidence of trust and generosity.
- Data has a story. Visuals should create a controlled path from context to insight to action, not a wall of charts.
- Accessibility is non-negotiable. Beauty serves inclusion; if they conflict, inclusion wins.
- Tugger expands the frame. Future AI strategy content should connect Power BI foundations to product, decision-making, and trustworthy AI adoption.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline. Body text, labels, controls, and placeholder text must meet contrast requirements in both light and dark mode. Keyboard navigation, focus states, semantic headings, alt text, and reduced-motion alternatives are required.

Motion should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, especially the 3D architect experience and any page-load choreography. Visitors should never be trapped behind animation, WebGL, audio, or a device capability check.

The tone should be welcoming without sanding off the personality. Community pages should make beginners, career switchers, end users, citizen developers, and experienced technical people feel equally allowed in the room.
